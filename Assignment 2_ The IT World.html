Autonomous Vehicles
What does it do?
An autonomous vehicle is a vehicle that has the ability to sense its outside
circumstance and navigate to predetermined destination without human input. It is
also known as a driverless vehicle or a self-driving car. Right now, all autonomous
vehicles have carried cameras and sensors that can collect data about a car’s
outside environment, and there is also an onboard software system inside that
processes the data and information gathered and decides where should the car go
and what reaction should the car take. At the moment, this new technology is still
under tested and not universal at all because it is not ready for the public yet. In
March of 2018, a driverless car for the test drives accidentally hit and killed a 49-
year-old woman in the public area at night and the vehicle did not appear to
response at all before the collision. Therefore, there is still a long way to go before
this technology is completely accepted by the wide public.
Some automation has already been existed for a long time. There are Anti-skid
Braking System (ABS), Cruise Control, Self Parking System, Automatic Braking
System, Lane Keeping Assistance (LKA) and many more. However, those
9
mentioned are more likely related to level 1 of self driving automation. There are 5
levels of self driving automation (from level 0 to level 5). Mercedes Benz has the
autonomous cruise control system which is called Distronic Plus now and it helps the
car to maintain a safe distance with the other cars on the road by adjusting its own
speed automatically. Tesla has the autopilot which allows full self-driving capacity of
all aspects of driving at SAE level 5, which is the highest safety level. Google has a
self-driving car which does not have a steering wheel, gas pedal, or brake pedal, and
is fully autonomous without human input. This prototype of car is still under tested.
Uber has done 2,000,000 miles in autonomous mode by 22 December, 2017 and the
car is still under tested. The engineers are figuring out some technologies which can
prevent self-driving collision. The 2018 Audi A8 Luxury Sedan is a commercial car
that has the ability to reach level 3, which has a function called Traffic Jam Pilot that
can take full control of everything and up to 60 kilometre per hour.
Many companies have announced that their fully self-driving cars are released in
three years. Aldi, Ford, Volkswagen, General Motor, BMW (inext), Toyota, Tesla,
Baidu, Nissan, and some other companies all make announcements that fully
autonomous cars can be available incredibly soon. Singapore will start its self-driving
taxi in Singapore’s 1 North District and it is aiming to push this to the other cities
worldwide.
Bayesian Simultaneous localisation and mapping (SLM) algorithms are used for the
driverless cars, and there are multiple sensors like lidar, GPS, stereo vision, IMU and
the offline map can estimate current location and the map updated. All the
information gathered by the sensors can improve self-driving experience, and the
technology has been used is called Sensor Fusion. There is also a neural network,
which is able to “learn” what action should the car take after encountering every
situation, and then this applied to the real life situations.

Blockchain and cryptocurrencies
What does it do?
The blockchain is basically a distributed database, which is the brainchild of a
person or group of people known by Satoshi Nakomoto who invented and released
the tech in 2009. The blockchain is a decentralized ledger of all transactions across
a peer to peer network. To use blockchain technology, transaction could be digitally
and anonymously confirmed without a requirement for central clearing authority by
participants. Cryptocurrency is a medium exchange, created and stored
electronically in the blockchain, using encryption techniques to control the creation of
monetary units and to verify the transfer of funds. Bitcoin is the best known example.
It is getting a much more competitive space regarding of blockchains. However,
Ethereum is holding up as state of the art in terms of developing distributed
applications. Transactions are relatively fast, cheap, and building usable applications
is not difficult. Using Numerai as an example, which is a hedge fund that
crowdsources its financial predictions without requiring its users to share their
models with the company. They use a cryptocurrency called Numeraire to let users
stake real value on their predictions - a higher stake, combined with good
predictions, leads to a higher return. The reason they use a real cryptocurrency is
that it makes the system trustless - their users don’t have to trust the company in
order to know that they will receive remuneration for their work + risk-taking.
17
Additionally, blockchain has potential applications far beyond bitcoins and
cryptocurrencies. For instance, customers could be use the blockchain to manage
fractional ownership in autonomous cars; in financial services, faster, cheaper
settlements could shave billions of dollars from transaction costs while improving
transparency; in voting, using a blockchain code, constituents could cast votes via

A blockchain database consists of blocks and transactions. Blocks contain batches
of transactions that are “hashed” and encoded. Each block contains the hash of the
block before it, which links the two and forms the chain. This process validates each
block, all the way back to the original, and is integral to the database’s security.
When a transaction takes place, its details are encrypted and a unique multiple-
character transaction number is generated. Instead of other users in the blockchain
being able to see the exact details of the transaction, this number is recorded in the
ledger as a placeholder. All the users of the network will be able to see that the
transaction has taken place but only the parties involved in the transaction can
access and view its details. All this makes any fraudulent activity easier to spot. An
external hacker would have to gain access to every computer that holds a copy of
the blockchain database, and at the same time, in order to tamper with it.
The essence of money is neither credit nor use value, but a consensus. The public
believes that gold, as a currency, can facilitate transactions and is willing to use gold
in transactions. That gold is money. The government can only stipulate that gold is
not a legal currency and it cannot stipulate whether gold is a currency. It is not the
credit that supports Bitcoin, but the consensus of the public. The public consensus is
based on the use of bitcoin introduced above, such as payment remittances, smart
contracts, information disclosure, hype, illegal use, value storage, currency issuance
and social equity. The public believes that Bitcoin can provide convenience for
transactions. It is a good currency, not only a currency, but even contracts can be
handled together (smart contracts). Then Bitcoin is used as a currency.

Translation device
What would the world be like without language barrier? You can travel and work in a
foreign country without the need to spend hours, months or even years of learning a
new language. You don’t need to hire a translator when meeting up with a client or
discussing business deals. You can make new friends, find love in a foreign country
and express your feelings to them while not knowing their language. Students can
talk to their classmates around the world and share knowledge. Children who were
raised in another country can talk to their parents or other family members in their
mother tongue language.
Some of the big IT companies have been developing translation programs to help
users battle the challenge called “language barrier”. In December 2014, the company
announced the limited release of Skype Translator, which can translate a
conversation between two people video chatting in different languages, in real time.
The software can handle English, Spanish, Italian, and Mandarin. Google also has a
smartphone app, free and available to the public, which can transcribe spoken text in
one language, translate it, and then speak the result aloud in another. Now there are
many applications available to use on mobile devices that help with translating texts
and speeches from one language to another, most requires Internet connection.
Translate devices have been on the market for few years now. Ili is a handheld
device that can translate speeches from English to Chinese, Japanese or Spanish in
less than a second and without the need of Wi-Fi. The problem ili have is that it’s a
one way device and have only one input which means it can only translate from
English speeches and not other languages, which makes it pretty much useless for
any situations because the person you’re talking to understand what you’re saying
but you don’t understand them. Wearable devices will also be available on the
market in 2018, which also have their pros and cons. The pros are that they are
more convenient to carry around and likely to be smaller than a handheld device,
and can translate two ways. The cons are that they either require an app to be
usable or a phone like the Pixel Buds by Google. Another con is that most of them
don’t work offline
We hope to create a device that will empower user to go and explore countries that
they have never been to before, meet new people, make friends and share their
cultures. A device that is lightweight and easy to use, able to translate in less than
one second and can translate two ways. It must be able to use offline for travellers
who like to explore remote areas or want to disconnect from the Internet for the
period they’re travelling. It may not require an app to use so the user don’t have to
always take out their phone and save on the battery. The use of device should not
require the person who the user is talking to owning the same device, as it’s not
likely that everybody is willing to buy a product that they might not use often or they
20
can’t afford it. It needs to have the ability to cancel out the background noise and
capture the correct voice and speech to translate. Another important thing is that it
should not be fragile so that the user can use it in most environment and weather.
