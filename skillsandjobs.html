Skills and Jobs

1. Product Manager

The first position must be the PM, product manager, who design the product and makes it come true. Product managers are responsible for directing the success of the product and leading the team to improve it. It is a very important role, especially in IT companies. They are in charge of setting strategy, roadmap, and features for a product or the whole product line.

There are two important skills out of many that a product manager should possess. The first is strategic thinking which starts with asking the right questions, then understanding the market and competition, and finally defining the product roadmap. The product manager must have the ability to predict how long each stage of the production cycle will take, place its product to take advantage in the market, and conceive strategies to make costs lower and control risks throughout the product life cycle.

The second skill is analytical skills that follow strategic thinking. This is about the research and analysis of the user data to make product decisions profitable. This is a skill which is driven by data rather than completely based on intuition. A product manager who has strong analytical skills understands how to use the data and form solutions for business strategy, developing products.

With regard to this project, the PM should relevant experience in creating AR or VR related projects.

2. Market Analyst

The second position should be the market analyst who works with product manager to define the product. To choose one person standing for the whole market department, then it would be market manager. The market analyst is a job position which studies and assess the market trends. This job is to determine what products the company should produce and how to sell them based on the market trends. Business strategies rely on market analysis, which applies to all industries that distribute products or services.

Strong mathematics and market analysis capabilities are definitely critical to market research analysts. They should have a deep understanding of the industry in which they are working and be able to speak in front of strangers and deliver results to team members and managers.

3. Technician

In our project, if chosen one person to represent the technical team, it would be architect of the project who is in charge of the technical implementation of the whole project. 'A software architect is a software expert who makes high-level design choices and dictates technical standards, including software coding standards, tools, and platforms. The leading expert is referred to as the chief architect.'(Wikipedia)

First of all, architects are technical leaders which means that in addition to technical skills, architects must also possess leadership qualities. So called leadership can be characterized by the position of the organization and the qualities exhibited by the person who takes this position.

As far as positions in the organization are concerned, the architect is the technical leader of the project and should have the right to make technical decisions. Because of their position, architects and product managers represent the public role of the project. As a team, they are the main point of contact for people outside the project.

As for this project, the architect should has relevant experience in developing VR software. Because the hardware and software of VR is totally different from the software running on the mobile platform and desktop machines.

4. Software Operations Manager

This position is about how to run and maintain the services of our product. Without an effective way to maintain an amazing product, it equals a failure. The Operations Manager is a senior position responsible for overseeing the production of goods and services. This is the job of an operations manager to make sure that the software can run effectively and provide smooth and usable services that meet the expectations and needs of customers. Operational managers have a wide range of roles, and specific responsibilities among different IT companies may vary, but they typically include analyzing and monitoring current production or help systems to check their effectiveness and formulate necessary improving strategies.

The first skill that software operation manager require is financial management Skills.

Understanding the financial performance of an organization is critical to operations managers. Operations managers should know how to forecasts, budgets, income statements, and balance sheets. The second skill is utilizing information systems. Operations managers use information and data can better solve business problems, initiate improvements, and support management.
